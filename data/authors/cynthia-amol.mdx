Cynthia Amol is a PhD student in Computer Science and a Natural Language Processing (NLP) researcher focusing on low-resource African languages. She is a Data Science Africa (DSA) Visiting Fellow at Sunbird AI and a Google PhD Fellow in NLP at Maseno University, where she researches speech technologies for Kenyan languages.
A recipient of the Deep Learning Indaba Alele-Williams Masters Award, Cynthia champions mentorship, collaborative learning, and inclusivity, seeing them as vital to empowering communities. She is keen on developing community-centred solutions using technology, believing strongly in investing in the community to build capacity and create localised, context-aware solutions for societal challenges.
As the Head of Data at Tonative, she oversees data validation across the various language groups and coordinates operations. She is central to performance monitoring and collaborations, and is a proud ambassador for the community
