Alfred Malengo Kondoro is a researcher in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Artificial Intelligence, with a strong focus on African language technologies and culturally grounded AI systems. Over the past several years, he has worked at the intersection of multilingual NLP, passive sensing, and real-world AI deployments, contributing to research on Swahili and other low-resource African languages, as well as large-scale benchmarks and multimodal datasets.

Alongside his academic work, Alfred leads community-driven research initiatives aimed at strengthening African representation in AI research and practice. He has contributed to peer-reviewed publications in international venues and actively collaborates with researchers across Africa, Europe, and Asia. His research sits at the intersection of language technology, longitudinal user behavior, and responsible AI, with a particular emphasis on designing inclusive systems that perform reliably in real-world, in-the-wild contexts.
