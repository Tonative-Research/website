Joy Naomi Olusanya is a linguist and a researcher in Natural Language Processing and Machine Learning with a focus on low-resource languages, machine translation, multilingual NLP, healthcare applications, and benchmark evaluation on culturally grounded datasets. She holds a BA in Linguistics from Obafemi Awolowo University.  She brings strong experience in community leadership and AI for social impact, previously serving as Workshop Chair for the Centering Low-Resource Languages and Cultures in the Age of Large Language Models (CLRLC–LLMs) Workshop @ NeurIPS 2025.

Currently, Joy serves as Training Manager, NLP Researcher, and Yoruba Language Lead at Tonative. She is also the Founder and CEO of the Center for Low-Resource Languages and Cultures, driving initiatives that bridge linguistic diversity with cutting-edge AI research.
