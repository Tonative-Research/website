# Global PIQA: Testing AI with Local Culture

AI models often learn from the same western sources. This makes them forget how people live in other parts of the world. Global PIQA is a new test for AI. It checks if models understand physical knowledge. This knowledge is about daily life, customs, and traditions.

A team of 335 researchers from 65 countries built this tool. It covers over 100 languages and 23 writing systems.

### Why Cultural Depth Matters

Many AI tests just translate English ideas into other languages. Global PIQA is different. More than half of the test uses local facts. It asks about specific foods and traditions. This ensures the test is truly local. It does not just copy western concepts.

### Results from the Research

We found that top AI models have big gaps. Some models are very weak in low-resource languages. The accuracy can drop by 37 percent. Open-source models usually perform worse than paid ones. This shows that AI still needs to learn about local communities.

### Our Work in Nigeria

The Tonative team focused on Nigerian Pidgin and Yoruba. We are native speakers and linguists. We wrote questions about Nigerian culture. We asked about how to prepare local food. We also included traditional practices.

This work helps our mission. We want to increase data for African languages. By adding high-quality data to Global PIQA, we make AI more inclusive. This helps future models understand the Nigerian context better.
